About

I'm an EECS student building at the intersection of hardware, software, and AI. I founded Vayu Systems to develop next-generation agentic operating systems and OCRadar to create AI-powered tools for early cancer detection.

For four years, I've led software and engineering at Oakton Robotics, developing autonomous control systems, vision pipelines, and sensor integration while mentoring students across CAD, electronics, and software.

My work has reached millions worldwide, featured by WTOP, Korea Times, United Airlines, and Washington Dulles International Airport. I earned National Merit distinction while completing 18 AP courses and won recognition at the DC region's largest high school hackathon.

Whether I'm designing custom PCBs, reverse-engineering chip architectures, or photographing aircraft at Dulles, I'm driven by curiosity and a commitment to making technology accessible.
