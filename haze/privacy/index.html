Privacy Policy

Last updated June 9, 2026

Haze is built to be quiet — and that goes for your data too. The app has no accounts, no ads, and no third-party tracking or analytics. This policy explains the little it does handle.

What Haze collects

Nothing is collected by me. Haze has no servers of my own, no login, and no analytics SDKs. It does not build a profile of you or sell, share, or monetize any information.

Location

If you grant location access, Haze uses your location only while you are using the app, and only to show the forecast for where you are. Your coordinates are used on your device and sent to the weather provider to retrieve that forecast. They are never stored on a server I control and are never linked to your identity.

Location is entirely optional. You can deny or revoke permission in iOS Settings and still use everything — just tap the search icon and enter any city to load its forecast.

Saved locations & settings

Your saved locations and preferences (such as °F/°C and mph/km·h) are stored locally on your device. Deleting the app removes them.

Weather data & third parties

Forecasts come from Open-Meteo, a free open weather API. To return a forecast, the app sends the relevant coordinates (your location or a city you search) to Open-Meteo. No API key, account, or personal identifier is attached. Open-Meteo's handling of requests is governed by its own terms and privacy practices.

Apple's privacy label

Consistent with the App Store privacy label, Haze may use precise or coarse location for app functionality only. This data is not linked to your identity and is not used to track you.

Children

Haze is rated 4+ and is suitable for all ages. It does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date above.
